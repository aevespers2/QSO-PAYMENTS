Separation of authority

Objects propose. Policies evaluate. Authorized actors approve.

Proposal→Intent→Validation→Authorization→Allocation→Disabled adapter boundary→Evidence

Intent

A bounded proposal identifies purpose, amount or unit, recipients, provenance, expiry, environment, and an idempotency key.

Authorization

An independent record identifies who approved what, under which limits and conditions, for which environment, and with what revocation state.

Settlement boundary

A separately approved adapter may eventually submit an authorized instruction. Adapters are disabled by default and documentation never proves production capability.

Record lifecycle

Each stage adds evidence instead of rewriting history.

Validate and authorize

Malformed, duplicate, expired, prohibited, or policy-incompatible intents fail closed. A valid schema remains only a candidate for authorization.

Allocate and reconcile

Routes must sum to the approved total under explicit rounding, remainder, cap, and priority rules. Any mismatch stops the process.

Record outcomes

Simulation results, adapter failures, receipts, and unresolved status are distinct records linked to the original intent and approval.

Dispute and recover

Contested evidence is preserved with reason, reviewer, status, and permitted remediation. Rollback restores the last verified publication or disables a separately approved adapter.

Explicit environment labels

Documentation is not simulation. Simulation is not production.

Documentation — current

Concepts, trust boundaries, risks, and review procedures. No working payment service is implied.

Simulation — future approval

Deterministic fictional calculations without external credentials, custody, signing, or transfers.

Testnet — separately governed

Non-production integration using isolated credentials only after contract, security, and operational approval.

Production — not authorized

An independently audited and legally reviewed deployment. It cannot be inferred from documentation, fixtures, schemas, or testnet activity.

Fail-closed by design

Ambiguity, missing evidence, and excess authority stop the flow.

No self-approval

A QSO cannot authorize its own proposal, infer permission from identity, or convert prior behavior into financial authority.

No repository secrets

Credentials, private keys, and sensitive account identifiers must not appear in intents, public artifacts, fixtures, or generated documentation.

No fabricated certainty

Receipts are adapter evidence with stated limitations. Unresolved or unknown status is preserved instead of being reported as final settlement.

Replay resistance

Every future executable contract requires idempotency keys, expiry, duplicate detection, bounded retry, and explicit reconciliation.

Privacy minimization

Payment metadata is minimized, classified, redacted, and excluded from public evidence unless publication is explicitly authorized.

Publication evidence

A documentation release requires reproducible Pages output, link and accessibility checks, claims review, security/privacy review, hashes, provenance, and rollback evidence.
