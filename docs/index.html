Quantum State Object Economic Layer

Auditable payment intent without uncontrolled custody.

QSO-PAYMENTS defines the contracts, records, and adapter boundaries needed for direct payments, revenue splits, royalties, validator rewards, escrow milestones, treasury allocations, storage fees, routing fees, and agent-execution credits.

Separation of authority

Objects propose. Policies authorize. Adapters settle.

Intent

A QSO emits a bounded payment intent describing purpose, amount, asset, recipients, provenance, and expiry.

Authorization

Policy gates validate approvals, limits, route totals, milestones, freeze points, and dispute state.

Settlement

An external adapter may execute an approved instruction and return a signed receipt. Adapters are disabled by default.

Fail-closed by design

Every economic action remains bounded and reviewable.

Immutable evidence

Intent, authorization, allocation, receipt, dispute, and settlement records are linked through append-only provenance.

Explicit environments

Simulation, testnet, and production are separate modes. Production transfer capability cannot be inferred from documentation or test fixtures.

Freeze and rollback

Missing approvals, invalid totals, expired intents, adapter errors, and policy mismatches stop processing before settlement.

No autonomous custody

QSOs do not hold private keys, sign transactions, or directly control external accounts.

QSO-PAYMENTS is an experimental, audit-first architecture. No page on this site constitutes a financial product, promise, or production settlement service.
